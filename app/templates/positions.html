Open Positions

Postdocs

The Computational Social Affective Neuroscience Lab seeks to recruit up to 3 postdoctoral researchers to begin Winter 2019 to work on federally-funded research investigating naturalistic elicitations of emotional experiences. Projects include collecting data via fMRI, intracranial EEG, and facial expressions. Ideal candidates will have: (1) a PhD in a relevant quantitative discipline (e.g., neuroscience, computer science, physics, applied math), (2) a strong background in signal processing and data analysis (e.g., computational modeling, time series analysis, machine-learning, computer vision), (3) a demonstrated record of creativity and innovation, & (4) an interest in contributing to the development of open source software (e.g., Python). Interested applicants should contact Dr. Luke Chang with a brief statement of interest (1 page maximum), a CV, and 2 letters of reference.

Graduate Students

Dr. Chang will not be accepting graduate students fall 2019

Research Assistants

The laboratory has openings for part-time volunteer research assistants to start Winter 2019. Primary responsibilities include assisting in the collection, processing, and analysis of behavioral, psychophysiological, and neuroimaging data. We require a minimum commitment of 10 hours per week for at least one quarter. Please email Dr. Chang for more information.
